About me


My name is Steven Zhang. I am currently in my second-year at SFU, studying Computing Science. Though I find the inner workings of computers to be fascinating, my time at SFU is only a stepping stone in my career as a military pilot in the Royal Canadian Air force. Having undergone a year-long screening and selection process during grade 12, I was selected shortly after graduating high school in July of 2023. The Canadian Armed Forces currently subsidizes my university education as part of its pipeline to train its pilots - and most of its officers for that matter.




My military career (so far)


"How do you attend SFU if you're in the military?"


Though I am a full-time member of the Canadian Armed Forces, I live a civilian life during the school year. I still get paid and receive benefits as any military member would, and I am still held to the same standards common to all members of the CAF. Upon graduation and receiving my commission, I will be posted to one of the many military bases across Canada for my flight training (likely in Manitoba). Until then, military training only occurs in the summer.


Basic Military Officer Qualification (BMOQ)


I did my BMOQ during the summer of 2024 in Saint-Jean-Sur-Richelieu, Quebec. In many ways, it was as one would imagine it to be: early mornings (days start at 0500), long, physical days, lots of push-ups, lots of running, a LOT of yelling, a LOT of swearing, and a LOT of sweating. The Canadian Military is known among its allies as being small but highly professional, and the training reflects that; the instructors genuinely wished to see us succeed, and applying stress through yelling and physical punishment, among other things, is what they were trained to do. Moreover, they were very nice people who were always willing to help, and often very funny and creative with their verbal "incentives". An experience such as BMOQ builds camaraderie like no other. For 12 weeks, I was laughing, sweating, suffering, and hurting with the same group of fine people.


The dream of flying

Though I do enjoy studying computer science, I see every day I spend at SFU as one step closer to sitting in the cockpit.

Things I find cool


Aviation


There are 4 phases of flight training for RCAF pilots (5 if you're becoming a fighter pilot). Phase 1 takes place in Portage la Prairie, Manitoba, on the Grob G 120. Phase 2 takes place in Moose Jaw, Saskatchewan, and students fly the CT-156 Harvard II. In some cases, students who are certain they do not want to fly fighter jets in their career can continue in Portage la Prairie on the Grob G 120 during Phase 2. It is at the end of Phase 2 where students will be streamed into one of the three aircraft types: helicopters, multi-engine, or jets; from here, training varies depending on the stream.


I would happy with any of the three streams, though I do have my preferences for particular aircraft. I have attached links below for the types of aircraft I would like to fly in my career, by aircraft type.


Helicopters


Currently, this is the stream I am most interested in, specifically Tactical Helicopters (Tac Hel). What drew me to Tac Hel was the nature of its mission set, which is to provide support to the Army. Canada flies two different helicopters in the tactical role: the CH-146 Griffon, which is my most desired aircraft currently, and the CH-147 Chinook, one of my least desired (ironically). At the core of Tac Hel is going low and fast to support troops on the ground which is what drew me to this community.
Canadian Griffons in Iraq


Multi-engine


In my eyes, this route is the safest yet least exciting, in the sense that large fixed-wing aircraft are the least maneuverable and usually do not fly at low altitudes, making it safer compared to helicopters and fighters; yet because they fly long missions, the flights are usually less exciting. Being experienced with multi-engine platforms would also provide me the necessary skillset to work in the commercial aviation industry if I were to pursue that route. There are three multi-engine platforms I would like to fly: the CC-130H/J Hercules (tactical airlift, air-to-air refueling, or search and rescue), the yet to be delivered CC-295 Kingfisher (search and rescue), and the yet to be delivered P-8A Poseidon (maritime patrol and reconnaissance).
